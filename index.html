This freshly renovated apartment occupies the entire second floor of a prewar Italian row house. It is situated on a quiet street in Ridgewood, 4 minutes from the Fresh Pond Road subway station.

The house sits at the end of the row, so it is free-standing along one side, allowing for windows in every room of the home—a rarity in row houses!

The master bedroom features large bay windows that overlook the quiet, tree lined street. It is a generous 176 sq ft. Attached, and also street-facing, is a smaller room (70 sq ft), which has its own closet, as well as a separate entrance. It is ideal for a nursery, kid’s room, or small home office.

 

 

 

On the other side of the master bedroom, pocket doors open up into a mid-sized room which has a generous two-tier closet along the side. This room is approximately 80 sq ft. It was previously used as a crafting room and walk-in closet.

 

 

 

Through that room is what is traditionally used as the dining room in such row houses (approximately 133 sq ft). The previous tenants have installed wide french doors between this room and the living room, to further separate the spaces, but if this is not for you, we’d be happy to help uninstall them.

 

 

 

The huge living room (211 sq ft), like the bedroom, has large bay windows that drench the room in light. Adjacent to the living room is the kitchen, which features a lovingly restored vintage MagicChef gas stove. Also newly installed in the kitchen is a Galanz retro-style fridge, a Bosch dishwasher, and a farmhouse kitchen sink along with dark wood countertops.

 

 

 

 

 

 

From the kitchen, you can step out onto a full-sized balcony that overlooks the backyard below. It can comfortably fit a patio dining set for two and a parasol.

 

 

 

 

The bathroom was renovated in December. It has a dark slate tile flooring and a cast iron claw-foot tub.
